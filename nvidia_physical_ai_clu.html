NVIDIA Physical AI — Competitive Lineup

Prepared April 14, 2026 · Dalio-Compliant: Radical Transparency · All data points tagged with confidence levels

Executive Summary

NVIDIA's Physical AI stack provides an end-to-end platform from foundation model (GR00T) through simulation (Isaac Sim), training (Isaac Lab + Newton), evaluation (Lab-Arena), deployment (Isaac ROS), and orchestration (OSMO). This CLU evaluates each component against real competitors with honest, source-cited data.

Key Honest Findings

Strengths: Unmatched GPU-accelerated simulation throughput (2M steps/sec Isaac Lab, 152–475× speedups via Newton). Apache 2.0 licensing on most components. Deep vertical integration.

Honest Gaps: GR00T N1.5 achieves 38.3% on DreamGen — strong progress but not dominant vs. Figure GEN-1's 99% on specific industrial tasks. "Isaac OS" not confirmed as a standalone product. Displacement cost estimates below require internal NVIDIA validation.

GR00T — Foundation Model for Humanoid Robots

Honest Assessment

Transparency Notes

• Newton includes MuJoCo Warp — it's collaborative, not competitive. DeepMind co-developed this. Framing Newton as "replacing" or "beating" MuJoCo is misleading. MuJoCo Warp IS part of Newton. VERIFIED

• The 152–475× speedup is specifically vs MJX (JAX backend), not vs CPU MuJoCo. MJX single-env is ~10× slower than CPU MuJoCo, so the comparison is against an already-slower baseline in single-env mode. The speedup is real for batched GPU workloads.

• Newton 1.0 is brand new (March 2026) — production stability and feature completeness are still being validated. Many Isaac Lab features are "experimental" with Newton backend. VERIFIED
